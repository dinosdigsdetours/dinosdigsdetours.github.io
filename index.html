Note: this project is still under development. If you have any questions or suggestions contact us at the email below.

Dinos Digs Detours is meant to be an easy to use, fun and educational site. The maps show the locations where fossils have been found. The links show images and descriptions of those fossils while the collections link directs you to the scientific database with more technical information. The dates are rough estimates. We try to be scientifically accurate, but don't guarantee this and sometimes choose clarity and simplicity over scientific precision.

Dinosaurs and Friends show: dinosauria, pterosauria, ichthyosauria, plesiosauria, mosasaurus, crocodylomorpha.

Young mammals show mammalia from 30 million to 1 million years ago.

Old mammals are mammalia older than 30 million years.

Trilobites are trilobita.

Basic Paleontology

Dinosauria: The clade containing the dinosaurs, including famous species like Tyrannosaurus rex and Triceratops.

Pterosauria: Flying reptiles, including Pteranodon and Quetzalcoatlus.

Ichthyosauria: Marine reptiles that looked similar to modern dolphins, such as Ichthyosaurus.

Plesiosauria: Long-necked marine reptiles like Elasmosaurus.

Mosasaurus: Large marine reptiles related to modern lizards and snakes.

Crocodylomorpha: The group including modern crocodiles and their extinct relatives.

Mammalia: The class of animals including all mammals, from ancient species to modern ones.

Trilobita: An extinct group of marine arthropods with a distinctive three-lobed body, including species like Asaphus and Phacops.

Orders vs Names: An order is a large group of animals, like Proetida which includes many different kinds of trilobites but not all of them. The information we give about fossils is taken from wikipedia for the fossil name, but when that is not available, we provide information about the 'order' which contains the species. For example "Griffithidella welleri" is found in the map but not in wikipedia, so its order Proetida is used since Griffithidella welleri is a type of Proetida.
